Ranger

Changes are In Bold

Favored Enemy

Beginning at 1st level, you have significant experience studying, tracking, hunting, and even talking to a certain type of enemy.

Choose a type of favored enemy: aberrations, beasts, celestials, constructs, dragons, elementals, fey, fiends, giants, monstrosities, oozes, plants, or undead. Alternatively, you can select two races of humanoid (such as gnolls and orcs) as favored enemies.

You have advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track your favored enemies, as well as on Intelligence checks to recall information about them.

When you hit one of your favored enemies with a weapon attack you do additional damage equal to your wisdom modifier.

When you gain this feature, you also learn one language of your choice that is spoken by your favored enemies, if they speak one at all.

You choose one additional favored enemy, as well as an associated language, at 6th and 14th level. As you gain levels, your choices should reflect the types of monsters you have encountered on your adventures.

Hide in Plain Sight

Starting at 10th level, you can spend 1 minute creating camouflage for yourself. You must have access to fresh mud, dirt, plants, soot, and other naturally occurring materials with which to create your camouflage.

Once you are camouflaged in this way, you can try to hide by pressing yourself up against a solid surface, such as a tree or wall, that is at least as tall and wide as you are. You gain a +10 bonus to Dexterity (Stealth) checks. as long as you remain there without moving or taking actions. Once you move or take an action or a reaction, you must camouflage yourself again to gain this benefit.Once you move more than 100 ft from your original location, or the terrain you are in changes you must camouflage yourself again to gain this benefit.

Foe Slayer

At 20th level, you become an unparalleled hunter of your enemies. Once on each of your turns, you can add your Wisdom modifier to the attack roll or the damage roll of an attack you make against one of your favored enemies. When you attack your favored enemy you can add your proficiency bonus to all weapon damage rolls against your favored enemy, and your wisdom bonus to your attack rolls. You can choose to use this feature before or after the roll, but before any effects of the roll are applied.

Ranger's Companion

At 3rd level, you gain a beast companion that accompanies you on your adventures and is trained to fight alongside you. Choose a beast that is no larger than Medium and that has a challenge rating of 1/2 or your ranger level divided by 6, rounded down or lower (appendix D presents statistics for the hawk, mastiff, and panther as examples). Add your proficiency bonus to the beast’s AC, attack rolls, damage rolls, and skills it is proficient in. For saving throws it uses its own bonus or your bonus, whichever is greater. Its hit point maximum equals its normal maximum or six times your ranger level, whichever is higher.

The beast obeys your commands as best as it can. It takes its turn on your initiative, though it doesn’t take an action unless you command it to. On your turn, you can verbally command the beast where to move (no action required by you). You can use your bonus action to verbally command it to take the Attack, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, or Help action. Once you have the Extra Attack feature, you can make one weapon attack yourself when you command the beast to take the Attack action.

While traveling through your favored terrain with only the beast, you can move stealthily at a normal pace.

If the beast dies, you can obtain another one by spending 8 hours magically bonding with another beast that isn’t hostile to you, either the same type o f beast as before or a different one.

Barbarian

Rage

In battle, you fight with primal ferocity. On your turn, you can enter a rage as a bonus action.

While raging, you gain the following benefits if you aren’t wearing heavy armor:

  • You have advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws.
  • When you make a melee weapon attack using Strength, you gain a bonus to the damage roll that increases as you gain levels as a barbarian, as shown in the Rage Damage column of the Barbarian table. Equal to 2 times your proficiency bonus.
  • You have resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage.

If you are able to cast spells, you can’t cast them or concentrate on them while raging.

Your rage lasts for 1 minute. It ends early if you are knocked unconscious or if your turn ends and you haven’t attacked a hostile creature since your last turn or taken damage since then. You can also end your rage on your turn as a bonus action.

Once you have raged the number of times shown for your barbarian level in the Rages column of the Barbarian table, you must finish a long rest before you can rage again.

Factotum

The Factotum is the ultimate seeker of knowledge. A student of all schools, the Factotum seeks any kind of knowledge, and uses everything she learns to her advantage. Her constant study allows the Factotum to display flashes of inspiration, filling any role necessary, if only for a short time. The Factotum is nothing if not versatile, although occasionally unfocused.

Above all, the Factotum is prepared for anything, any circumstance and any challenge, at least when they manage to make it out of the library. While the Factotum's focus on learning new things is similar to the wizard's focus on arcane knowledge, the Factotum does not share the Wizard's focus. The Factotum is just as interested in that tome on arcane combat styles, or the architects detailed schematics as that previously unknown spell.

Factotum's as civilians often work as librarians, scientists, or advisors to people of power. As adventurers they travel the world seeking out stores of hidden knowledge, documenting unusual creatures, learning from reclusive masters of various arts and skills, or just meeting unusual people, enjoying foods they could never experience at home, and seeing the sights. In all of their doings they have a talent for coming up with the solution no one else thought of, and with a flash of inspiration, snatching victory and success from the jaws of failure and defeat.

Humans and half-elves are the most common factotums, followed by halflings and gnomes.

Proficiencies

Tools: Thieves' Tools

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

Class Features

Inspiration

The Factotum gets a number of inspiration points that they can spend to activate certain class features. They get inspiration points equal to their intelligence modifier plus half their class level(rounded down), minimum 1. They recover all of their inspiration points when they finish a short rest.

Inspired Skills

You can spend an inspiration point when making any skill check to add your intelligence bonus to the check.

Inspired Spellcasting

Starting at 1st level, you can spend inspiration points to cast spells. You know cantrips and spells according to the factotum table, and can learn any spell from any classes spell list as long as you are able to learn spells of that level. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for these spells. You can cast a cantrip as long as you have an inspiration point to spend on it, and a spell of a higher level as long as you have sufficient inspiration points to cast it, and a spell slot remaining. Casting a spell costs a number of inspiration points equal to 1 + the spells level.

Inspired Attack

Starting at 2nd level, you can spend an inspiration point before making an attack roll to add your intelligence bonus to the attack roll.

Inspired Damage

Starting at 2nd level, as a bonus action you can spend an inspiration point to add 1d8 to any weapon or spell damage roll you make until the start of your next turn.

Factotum Philosophy

At 3rd level your studies begin to focus on a particular specialty. Choose one of the Factotum Philosophies below, you gain different abilities depending on which philosophy you choose. You gain additional philosophy features at 10th and 17th level.

Ability Score Improvement

At 4th level, and again at 6th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you gain a +2 bonus to one ability score, +1 to two ability scores, or a feat.

Extra Attack

At 5th level you can make 2 attacks when you take the attack action on your turn.

Inspired Action

At 11th level, On your turn you can spend 3 inspiration points to get an extra action. You can only do this once per long rest.

Improved Inspired Damage

At 13th level, when you use the inspired damage feature you add 2d8 to your damage rolls.

Inspired Reaction

At 14th level, when you have the opportunity to react to an action but have already reacted that turn you can spend 2 inspiration points to react a second time. After you do this you cannot use this feature or the inspired action feature until the end of your next turn.

Inspired Recovery

At 20th level you no longer require a short rest to regain your inspiration points, but can do so by spending 10 minutes reading and studying your books and materials.

Factotum Philosophies

Brains Over Brawn

Those who follow the Brains over Brawn philosophy have focused their studies on the weaknesses of their potential enemies, taking the idea of knowledge as power into the field of combat. These scholars always seem to know their enemies weaknesses, and can use that knowledge to get around their defenses.

Know Your Enemy

Starting at 3rd level, you can spend a bonus action to study an opponent and discover how to get past its defenses. When you use this ability pick a target, you can use your intelligence modifier instead of strength or dexterity on weapon attack rolls and damage rolls until the start of your next turn.

Sneak Attack

Starting at 7th level, your knowledge of your opponents weaknesses allows you to strike with uncanny precision if you catch them off guard. Once per round, when you successfully hit an opponent with an attack and that enemy is adjacent to an ally or you have advantage against them, you can spend 1 inspiration point to add 2d6 damage to the attack roll, this increases to 3d6 at 11th level, 4d6 at 15th level, and 5d6 at 19th level.

Fighting Style Mimic

At 10th level the Brains over Brawn Factotum can pick one of the following fighting styles. Whenever they finish a long rest they can spend 3 inspiration points to replace this fighting style with a different one from the list. This ability does not stack with the fighting style granted by any other class.

  • Archery - You gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls you make with ranged weapons.
  • Dueling - When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapons, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls with that weapon.
  • Two-Weapon Fighting - When you engage in two-weapon fighting, you can add your ability modifier to the damage of the second attack.
  • Great Weapon Fighting - When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack you make with a melee weapon that you are wielding with two hands, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll, even if the new roll is a 1 or a 2. The weapon must have the two-handed or versatile property for you to gain this benefit.

Improved Inspired Attack

At 15th level, when you use an inspiration point to add a bonus to your attack roll, you can make an additional attack as a bonus action, adding your intelligence modifier to both attacks.

Arcane Dilettante

The Arcane Dilettante has studied magic enough that they begin to seem less like their faking it, and more like a practiced wizard. They no longer rely on bursts of inspiration for their most practiced spells, and can put together an idea of how to cast even unpracticed spells with less effort.

Improved Inspired Spellcasting

At 3rd level, the number of inspiration points you need to cast a spell is reduced by one, to a minimum of 0 for cantrips. Also you can cast an additional 2 spells per day (in addition to the amount listed in the Factotum table) before requiring a long rest, and can cast higher level spells, use the table below instead of the Factotum table to determine the highest level of spell you can cast and your spells known.

Additional Skill Proficiency

At 3rd level the arcane dilettante gains proficiency in the arcana skill. If they were already proficient in the arcana skill they gain expertise in the arcana skill (can add their proficiency bonus twice).

Spell Study

Starting at 7th level you have become a proficient enough spell caster that you can make a spell work with only minimal study. Starting when they gain this feature, the Factotum can add any spell they successfully observe being cast to a spell journal. In order to successfully add it to their spell journal the following conditions must be met:

  • The spell must be of a level the Factotum can cast.
  • The Factotum must be able to see(if it has somatic/material components) and hear(if it has a verbal component) the spell being cast.
  • They then must succeed on an intelligence (arcana) check with a DC equal to 15 + the spells level at the time the spell is cast. If they fail this check they can try again only if they observe the spell being cast again.

The Factotum can, in addition to their normal spells known, prepare 1 spell from their spell journal. Whenever you regain your spent inspiration points you can change what spell you have prepared.

Cantrip Master

Starting at 10th level, whenever The Factotum finishes a long rest they can trade out one cantrip you know for a different cantrip that they have successfully recorded to their spell journal.

Sage

Starting at 15th level you regain your spells per day after a short rest instead of a long rest.

Chameleon

The Chameleon focuses his studies on taking on any roll in society, to the point of literally being other people.

Additional Skill Proficiency

At level 3, when you take this philosophy, the factotum becomes proficient in the deception skill and with disguise kits if they are not already proficient.

Everday Illusions

At level 3, when you take this philosophy, the factotum learns the minor illusion cantrip in addition to their normal cantrips known, and can cast it without spending an inspiration point.

Hide in the Crowd

Starting at 3rd level, you can use an action to disappear into a crowd. Once you are camouflaged in this way, you can try to hide by standing within 5 feet of 2 or more other humanoid creatures. You gain a +5 bonus to Dexterity (Stealth) checks as long as you remain within 5 feet of at least 2 other humanoid creatures. If you take an action besides hide, your camouflage is revealed to the creatures you are with as well as anyone actively searching for you. If you are discovered you cannot attempt this action again for 1 minute. This bonus does not stack with other bonuses to Dexterity (Stealth) magical or otherwise.

Imposter

At 7th level, you gain the ability to unerringly mimic another person’s speech, writing, and behavior. You must spend at least three hours studying these three components of the person’s behavior, listening to speech, examining handwriting, and observing mannerisms.

Your ruse is indiscernible to the casual observer. If a wary creature suspects something is amiss, you have advantage on any Charisma (Deception) check you make to avoid detection.

If you spend an additional day and 10gp worth of materials you can also assemble the perfect disguise that when donned allows you to be indiscernible from your target to the casual observer.

Disguise Self

At 7th level you learn the spell Disguise Self if you do not already know it, and can cast it once per day without spending inspiration points or a spell slot.

Master of Deception

At 15th level you add your intelligence bonus to all stealth and deception checks. If you spend an inspiration point on a stealth or deception check you instead add double your intelligence bonus.

Major Image

At 15th level you also learn the spell Major Image if you do not already know it, and can cast it once per day without spending inspiration points or a spell slot.

The Skill Monkey

The Skill Monkey has made it their mission to master every skill, taking the Factotum's philosophy of prepared for anything.

Jack-of-all-Trades

Starting at 3rd level, you can add half your proficiency bonus, rounded down, to any ability check you make that doesn’t already include your proficiency bonus.

Improved Inspired Skills

Starting at 7th level, when the Skill Monkey uses the inspire skill ability they add their proficiency bonus as well as their intelligence modifier to the check, even if they are already proficient in that skill.

Expertise

At 10th level, choose two of your skill proficiencies. Your proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check you make that uses either of the chosen proficiencies.

Reliable Talent

By 15th level, you have refined your chosen skills until they approach perfection. Whenever you make an ability check that lets you add your proficiency bonus, you can treat a d20 roll of 9 or lower as a 10.

Proficiencies

Weapons: Simple and martial weapons
Saving Throws: Strength, Wisdom
Skills: Pick 2 skills from acrobatics, athletics, arcana, history, insight, religion, perception.

Sense Magic

At second level a scourer’s senses are specifically honed to detect magic within the area. As an action you can sense magic as if you were casting the detect magic spell.

Fighting Style

You adopt a particular style of fighting as your specialty. Choose one o f the following options. You can’t take a Fighting Style option more than once, even if you later get to choose again.

Archery You gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls you make with ranged weapons.

Great Weapon Fighting When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack you make with a melee weapon that you are wielding with two hands, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll, even if the new roll is a 1 or a 2. The weapon must have the two-handed or versatile property for you to gain this benefit.

Protection When a creature you can see attacks a target other than you that is within 5 feet of you, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll. You must be wielding a shield.

Two-Weapon Fighting When you engage in two-weapon fighting, you can add your ability modifier to the damage of the second attack.

Negate Magic

At level 3, a Scourer has learned to deny magic its full effect, and can do so even when it affects their mind. You have advantage on saving throws against magical fear and charm effects that you can see.

Disrupt Magic

At level 6, when an enemy within 5 feet of you attempts to cast a spell, you may take an opportunity attack against them, if you hit, the target must succeed on a concentration save or they fail to cast the spell and lose that action.

Ignore Magic

At level 7, The Scourer's quest is not slowed by magic as they have learned to continue through its grasp untouched. You have advantage against being blinded by magical affects you can see as well as removing magically created blindness from yourself. You are also unhindered by magically created difficult terrain and your speed cannot be reduced to 0 by magic. Your speed can spell be reduced to 0 through other magical effects which restrain, paralyze or incapacitate you.

Capture Magic

At level 10 you have learned how spell casters create and spin their magical effects, and have learned how to prevent it. When you successfully grapple a creature, the target becomes unable to cast spells that have verbal or semantic components as long as they are grappled.

See Magic

At level 11, you have learned to see past the trickery magic can create. You have advantage on wisdom saves against spells and advantage on detecting illusions.

Evade Magic

When you succeed on a dexterity saving throw against a spell, you take no damage from the spell. If you fail the saving throw you only take half damage.

Resist Magic

At level 20 your training has made your body naturally resistant to most spells and magic. You are resistant to all magical damage and magical weapons except psychic.

Mentalities

A Scourer's journey begins with a certain mentality in mind. Do you despise all magic and wish to rid the world of its corrupting evils? Or do you fight fire with fire and use your own magic to nullify others?

Mage Slayer

Slay Magic

At first level your skills reveal weak spots in spell casters that others miss. Gain an extra 2d8 damage against an enemy that you have seen casting a spell within the last minute. This damage increases to 3d8 at level 5 and 4d8 at level 11 and 5d8 at level 17.

Ready for Combat

Gain proficiency in heavy armor.

Shield Breaker

At level 5 you have studied the effects of magical armor and know how to attack around it. Your attacks Ignore ac granted by magical effects such as mage armor or the shield spell.

Irresistable Attack

At level 9, your attacks are virtually unaffected by magic. Your attacks ignore any magically created effect that would offer them resistance to your damage such as the stoneskin spell. Spell that prevent you from attacking them at all, such as Otiluke's Resilient Sphere, still do so.

Engage Magic

Your desire to fight magic allows you to ignore the bodyguards they often keep. You can use disengage as a bonus action as long as you move towards an enemy that you have seen casting a spell within the last minute while doing so.

Improved Capture Magic

At level 13 your ability to restrain spell casters has been mastered. Your capture magic trait can be used on two creatures. Each creature must be grappled individually with an action. Captured targets have disadvantage against escape. If you are grappling two creatures at once, you are restrained.

Mage Hunter

At level 17 you have learned how to effectively fight spell casters to the point where they are easy prey for your skills. Your attacks have advantage against enemies that you have seen casting a spell within the last minute.

Counter Magic

Absorb Magic

At first level you have honed your senses to absorb and enemy's magic within you. Whenever you take damage from a spell, gain 5 temporary Hitpoints, this effect increases to 10 at level 5, 15 at level 11 and 20 at level 17. If you are reduced to 0 hitpoints by the damage of the spell this effect does not trigger.

Counter Magic

At level 5 you have learned the inner working of spells and can inherently use the spell against itself. Twice per short rest, you can bend another’s spell to your will. You can spend your reaction to negate or control another casters spell. 3rd level or lower spells are automatically affected while higher level spells require a dexterity check with the DC being 10 + the spells level. If you succeed, you can choose to negate the spell entirely, or if the spell targets a single target other than the caster, you can choose a different target for the spell. If the spell is a concentration spell, you take over concentration for the spells duration if you choose to do so. You may only concentrate on one spell at a time. This effect increases to three at 9th level and four at 14th level.

Dodge Magic

At level 9 Your reflexes have improved enough against magic to anticipate its affects. Once per long rest, when you are required to make a spell save against a spell you can see, you can spend your reaction and choose instead to succeed on the save before making the roll.

Disperse Magic

At level 13 you are able to disperse ongoing magical effects with ease. You can spend an action to disperse magic as if you were casting dispel magic at the 7th level. Higher level spells can be dispersed by making a dexterity check where the DC is 10 + the spells level.

Reveal Magic

Your senses extend far beyond normal creatures. You get 60ft true sight, advantage on all saves against magical effects that you can see, and you can always react to spells you can see being cast as long as you are not incapacitated.
